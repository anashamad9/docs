Atmet is an AI workspace built for teams that want to automate the repetitive work that slows them down. Instead of stitching together no-code tools or writing custom scripts, you describe what you want to automate in plain language — Atmet's AI agent figures out the steps, connects to the apps you already use, and gets the work done. From routing leads in HubSpot to summarising GitHub pull requests in Slack, Atmet turns business processes into reliable, repeatable automations without writing a single line of code.

<Note>
  Atmet is currently in **early access alpha**. Features and behaviour may change as we refine the product. If you run into anything unexpected, please share feedback with the team — your input directly shapes what gets built next.
</Note>

## Key concepts

Understanding five core concepts is all you need to get productive in Atmet.

### Workspaces

A workspace is the top-level container for everything your team does in Atmet — automations, skills, connected apps, and members all live inside a workspace. You can belong to multiple workspaces (for example, one for your company and one for a client project), and switching between them is instant.

### AI Core

AI Core is the chat interface at the heart of Atmet. Open it to describe an automation in plain language, ask questions about your connected apps, or instruct the agent to run a task right now. The AI agent understands context from previous messages in the conversation, so you can refine your request iteratively without starting over.

### Workflows

Workflows are the automations Atmet builds and runs on your behalf. Each workflow consists of a trigger (something that kicks it off, such as a new email or a scheduled time) and a sequence of actions (such as creating a Notion page or posting a Slack message). You can review, edit, enable, and disable workflows at any time from the Workflows dashboard.

### Skills

Skills are reusable building blocks that you can compose into workflows. A skill encapsulates a specific capability — such as "summarise this text" or "look up a contact in HubSpot" — and can be called by the AI agent or embedded in multiple workflows. Building a library of skills means the AI gets smarter about your specific processes over time.

### Apps (Integrations)

Apps are the services Atmet connects to in order to read data and take actions on your behalf. Atmet supports a growing catalogue of integrations including Gmail, Slack, Notion, HubSpot, GitHub, Jira, and more. You connect an app once per workspace, and every workflow and skill in that workspace can then use it.
