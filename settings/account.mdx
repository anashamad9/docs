Your Account settings control everything tied to your individual identity on Atmet — your name, photo, contact details, the job role the platform displays alongside your name, and the personalization answers the AI uses to tailor its responses. To open Account settings, click **Settings** in the bottom-left corner of the sidebar, then select **Account** from the left panel.

## Profile details

The top of the Account settings panel shows your current avatar, display name, and job role. Every field here — except your email address — can be edited and saved at any time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Account settings">
    Click **Settings** in the bottom-left of the sidebar. The settings panel opens. Select **Account** if it is not already active.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit your name">
    Update the **First name** and **Last name** fields. Your full name appears in the members list, in chat messages, and anywhere your identity is shown to teammates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your profile photo">
    Hover over your avatar in the top-left of the panel. Click the edit icon that appears, then choose **Upload image** to select a new photo from your device, or **Delete image** to remove the current one and revert to your initials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a phone number">
    Enter your phone number in the **Phone number** field. Use the international format (for example, `+1 555 000 0000`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your job role">
    Open the **Role** dropdown and pick the option that best describes your position — Operations Manager, Product Manager, Software Engineer, Designer, Marketing Manager, or Other. If you select **Other**, an additional text field appears so you can type a custom title.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save** at the bottom-right of the panel. The button is enabled only when you have unsaved changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Email address

Your email is shown in the panel but is **read-only** — it is tied to your login credentials and cannot be changed directly in Account settings. To update your login email, contact Atmet support.

### User ID

Your User ID is a unique identifier that appears at the bottom of the profile form. It is read-only. Click the copy icon next to it to copy it to your clipboard — useful when you need to reference your account in a support request or API call.

## Password reset

Atmet uses email-based password reset. There is no in-app password field.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request a reset link">
    In the **Password** section, click **Send a link**. Atmet sends a password-reset email to the address on your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow the email link">
    Open the email and click the reset link. You are taken to a page where you can set a new password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you do not see the email within a few minutes, check your spam or junk folder. The reset link expires after a short period — if it has expired, return to Account settings and request a new one.
</Tip>

## AI personalization

The **Personalization** section lets you store context about yourself so the AI can tailor its tone, style, and suggestions to your needs. These answers are saved locally in your browser and are not shared with other workspace members.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="About me" icon="user">
    Write a short summary about yourself — your role, the industry you work in, or anything you want the AI to keep in mind when helping you.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Communication style" icon="message-lines">
    Describe how you prefer the AI to communicate with you. For example: concise bullet points, mixed Arabic and English, always include worked examples.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Preferences" icon="sliders">
    Add any other standing instructions — formatting preferences, topics to avoid, tools you rely on, or default assumptions you want the AI to make.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Fill in any or all three fields, then click **Save**. The AI reads these answers at the start of each new conversation in AI Core.

<Note>
  Personalization preferences are stored in your browser's local storage. Clearing your browser data or switching devices will reset them. Keep a copy if you have written detailed answers you want to reuse.
</Note>

## Danger zone — deleting your account

The **Danger Zone** section at the bottom of the Account panel contains the option to permanently delete your account.

<Warning>
  Deleting your account is irreversible. All workspaces you own — and all data inside them, including chats, workflows, files, and integrations — will be permanently removed. Workspace members will lose access immediately. There is no recovery option after deletion is confirmed.
</Warning>

To delete your account, click **Delete my account** in the Danger Zone section and confirm the action in the dialog that follows. If you only want to leave a workspace without deleting your account entirely, go to **Members** settings and remove yourself from that workspace instead.
